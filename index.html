Accrued Revenue

Accrued revenue is a core revenue recognition concept for subscription, SaaS, and service-based businesses. This page explains what it is, why it matters, and how it appears in your accounting.

What is accrued revenue?

Accrued revenue is income that a business has already earned but hasn’t been paid for yet. You’ve delivered the product or service, but the customer hasn’t paid — at least not yet.

Even though the cash hasn’t arrived, that income is still recorded as revenue in your books because the work is complete and the customer owes the amount.

Why does accrued revenue matter?

Accrued revenue keeps your financial reporting honest. It recognizes revenue when it’s earned — not only when the payment hits your bank account.

This is important for understanding true profitability, planning cash flow, and reporting performance accurately. It’s especially relevant for companies that run on subscriptions or ongoing services.

Is accrued revenue an asset?

Yes. In accounting terms, accrued revenue is considered an asset because it represents money that will come in later.

It sits on the balance sheet under accounts receivable until payment is actually collected from the customer.

Accrued revenue vs deferred revenue

Accrued revenue and deferred revenue behave in opposite ways:

Accrued revenue: You’ve delivered the work already, and you’re waiting for payment. You record it as earned revenue now.

Deferred revenue: The customer has already paid, but you haven’t delivered the product or service yet. You can’t count it as earned revenue until delivery happens.

Both are important for cash flow visibility, especially in ecommerce and subscription businesses.

Example of accrued revenue

Imagine you run a subscription service and invoice a client for a 3-month plan that starts today.

You don’t wait until the end of the 3 months to book all that income. Instead, you record one-third of the revenue each month as service is delivered, even if the full payment comes later.

That monthly portion is accrued revenue: earned now, collected later.
